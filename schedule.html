Tournament Structure

The tournament will be composed of two stages as follows, with the caveat that actual format is subject to player turnout and other factors.


Open Stage

At this stage, all players will have the opportunity to compete in five to six four-player matches (three-player matches will be used as
necessary based on headcount). At the outset of each round of matches, players will receive a random playing card. The playing card
will determine the participants in each match as well as the station at which that match will occur. Each station will have its own set of
Dominion cards with most stations being a blend of two different sets as shown below. Players are free to play in as many or as few
Open Stage matches as they choose. However, participation in as many as possible is highly encouraged insofar as advancement to
the Playoff Stage will be based on total points accumulated during the Open Stage.


Playoff Stage

The concept behind the Playoff Stage is to advance the top 40% - 50% of the Open Stage field to a series of (probably about 3) four-
player elimination matches. The exact structure of the Playoff Stage will be determined on the day of the event based on actual
headcount and score distribution from the Open Stage. This stage will culminate in a four-player final table to see who wins the trophy
and which of you bitches comes in second.

Scoring

Finishing position in each game will be determined using the following rules:

1. Highest number of Victory Points
2. Latest in turn order. There are no ties.

Players will receive Advancement Points based on their performance in each game. In a four-player match, Advancement Points
shall be assigned as follows: 8/5/2/0. For a three-player match: 8/5/0. These scoring rules match the 2015 WBC format.


Miscellaneous Rules

1. Dominionathon is a gentleman's game. All rules should be interpreted in that light.

2. Tournament rules trump the rulebook. Otherwise, the rulebook controls.

3. The members of each table shall be responsible for officiating disputes that may arise from time to time regarding the application
of any rules. To the extent a dispute cannot be resolved amicably within the table, recourse may be had to a tournament organizer,
whose judgement shall be final. Given the need to keep the tournament moving, this remedy should be used sparingly.

4. All players are responsible for keeping the tournament moving at an acceptable pace. All efforts should be made to limit match
length to 45 minutes, particularly during the Open Stage. If a match is still ongoing at the 45-minute mark, an additional 5 minute
grace-period shall be given. After the expiration of the grace-period, the game is deemed over, scores are computed, and a winner
is assigned.

5. At the end of each match, the winner should fill out the scorecard (which all players should sign) and the winner is then responsible
for placing that scorecard in the official Scorecard Box at the official Scorer's Table. Failure to do so may result in those points
being lost forever.

6. All players are responsible for keeping track of those stations at which he/she has already played. If and when a player receives
a random station assignment that coincides with a station he/she has already played, he/she should request a different card.
This is the only acceptable basis for requesting a different station assignment. For purposes of keeping things moving, the
player must request a different assignment card at the time the original assignment card is issued or forfeit his/her right to do so.

7. Some expansions (e.g., Prosperity, Dark Ages, etc.) have "optional" cards (e.g., Colonies, Platinums, Shelters, etc.). If these
cards are present at your station, you should play with them; if they are not present, you should not.

8. Every effort will be made to comply with these rules. However, the organizer reserves the right to amend these rules consistent
ith the spirit of healthy competition as the tournament progresses on a case-by-case basis. To the extent an amendment may
be necessary, input will be solicited, but the organizer's decision is final.
